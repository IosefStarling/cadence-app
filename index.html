From basic to native.

Every phrase you used this week that sounds translated, generic, or B1-flat. With native rewrites and the reason behind each one.

Translated from Spanish
Spoken Mon · 14:22
"I have 5 years working in accounting"
I've been working in accounting for 5 years most natural
I've spent 5 years in accounting slightly formal
I'm five years into my accounting career conversational
Why it sounds translated: Spanish uses "tengo X años haciendo Y" (tener + duration). English flips this — it uses present perfect continuous because the action started in the past and continues. "I have X years working" is a literal translation that immediately marks you as a Spanish speaker.
Too basic for the room
Spoken Wed · 09:15
"The meeting was very good"
The meeting went really well neutral pro
The meeting was productive specific
We got a lot out of that meeting casual
Why "good" weakens you: In professional English, "good" is a placeholder. Native speakers pick a precise word: productive, smooth, solid, useful, on point. "Very good" doubles down on the same flat word — it shrinks your credibility in a meeting.
Calque pattern
Spoken Thu · 16:48
"Make me a favor and send the report"
Do me a favor and send the report corrected
Could you send the report over? more polished
Mind sending me the report? native casual
The hacer/make trap: Spanish uses "hacer" for almost everything (hacer un favor, hacer una pregunta, hacer una fiesta). English splits these: do a favor, ask a question, throw a party. "Make" collocates with creation only — make dinner, make a decision, make a mess.
